# Department Mandates

These mandates expand the canonical strategy doc without bloating it.

## Executive Leadership

Set direction, protect focus, allocate resources, and keep the company aligned to the Agent Engine.

Executive leadership should spend time on leverage and decisions, not execution rescue.

## Recruiting

Build predictable agent inflow at the pace required by the business math.

Recruiting is a core growth function, not a side responsibility.

## Sales Leadership

Protect agent productivity and maintain the production assumptions the growth model depends on.

Sales leadership protects the math. It should not be forced to compensate for recruiting failure.

## Retention

Keep strong agents, reduce avoidable churn, and intervene early when capacity is at risk.

## Marketing

Create leverage for recruiting, productivity, and retention through authority, visibility, proof, and reusable brand assets.

Marketing supports recruiting. It does not replace recruiting.

The `EVERYWHERE` concept belongs here as part of the marketing mandate.

## Operations

Remove friction, convert recruited agents into active capacity quickly, and make execution cleaner as the organization scales.

Operations supports growth. It should not hide upstream failures.

## Finance

Protect margin, validate assumptions, and keep growth real rather than inflated by weak economics.
