# Governance

This document defines how Benson Crew runs the business, makes decisions, and protects focus.

The goal is simple:

- fewer meetings
- clearer decisions
- faster execution
- no heroics
- no confusion

## Operating Lens

The business runs through one operating lens:

- ATTRACT
- GROW
- RETAIN

Leadership meetings start with the Agent Engine.

Departments respond to the engine.

Decisions follow.

If something does not clearly connect to Attract, Grow, or Retain, it does not belong in leadership meetings.

## What Leadership Meetings Are For

Leadership meetings exist to:

- surface reality early
- focus on the real constraint
- identify and remove cross-department bottlenecks
- make clear decisions
- assign ownership
- prevent escalation and heroics

They exist to run the business, not to talk around it.

## What Leadership Meetings Are Not For

Leadership meetings are not for:

- status updates
- task management
- tactical redesign in the room
- re-litigating old decisions
- rescuing work that should be owned elsewhere

Execution lives with department owners and Operations. Governance exists to keep execution aligned and focused.

## Cadence

### Weekly Leadership Execution Meeting

Purpose:
- run the business week to week using the Agent Engine
- surface current constraints
- remove bottlenecks
- make decisions

Focus:
- reality
- constraints
- decisions
- ownership

### Monthly Leadership Strategic Review

Purpose:
- step out of weekly execution
- review trends, culture, system health, and backlog sequencing

Focus:
- patterns
- direction
- strategic adjustment

### Department 1:1 and Functional Reviews

Purpose:
- review department health outside the leadership room
- coach the owner on KPI movement, blockers, and execution quality
- resolve local issues before they escalate upward

Focus:
- KPI movement
- blockers
- owner support

### Quarterly Planning and Reset

Purpose:
- review the prior quarter
- lock the next priorities
- reset leadership focus

Focus:
- what matters next quarter
- what stops
- what gets carried forward

## Roles

### Strategic Leadership

Strategic leadership exists to:

- set priorities and outcomes
- align the room
- confirm decisions
- remove true blockers

Strategic leadership should not become the default owner of execution detail.

### Department Owners

Department owners exist to:

- represent their function
- explain results using KPIs tied to Attract, Grow, or Retain
- surface blockers and dependencies
- commit to decisions once made

Department owners bring numbers, context, and plans. They do not bring task lists and ask leadership to manage them.

### PM / Operating Support

The PM role exists to:

- capture decisions
- track owners and due dates
- follow up between meetings
- bring operational context when needed

## Dashboard Review Cadence

Benson Crew operates on three dashboard layers:

### Agent Engine Dashboard

Primary scoreboard.

- reviewed weekly
- measures Attract, Grow, Retain, and financial output

### Department Health Dashboards

Diagnostic layer.

- reviewed in 1:1s and department reviews
- used to explain movement in the engine
- used by leadership only when a pillar is yellow or red

### Service and Experience Dashboards

Quality-control layer.

- reviewed by Operations and Retention
- used to protect service quality and brand trust

Leadership does not manage dashboards. Leadership uses dashboards to make decisions.

## Strategic Backlog

Any idea, initiative, or request that does not clearly support a current quarterly priority belongs in the Strategic Backlog.

The backlog should be:

- visible
- ranked
- reviewed monthly or quarterly
- activated only when it clearly supports Attract, Grow, or Retain

This is how focus is protected.

## Calendar and System Enforcement

This cadence should not live only in a document.

The system should:

- check the calendar for the required weekly, monthly, quarterly, and 1:1 cadences
- flag when a required meeting is missing or slips
- surface the right dashboard and review materials before each meeting
- log the decisions and follow-ups that come out of each cadence

## Governance Rules

- The Agent Engine speaks first.
- No other dashboards lead unless the engine shows a yellow or red issue.
- Departments respond with KPIs.
- Bottlenecks are surfaced openly.
- Decisions are made once and enforced.
- Weekly meetings run execution.
- Monthly meetings adjust strategy.
- Quarterly meetings reset priorities.
- Focus is protected by sequencing, not by saying yes to everything.
