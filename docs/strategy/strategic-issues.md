# Strategic Issues

Structural findings that explain why the current quarter priorities exist. These are design problems, not effort problems.

## Current Issues

**1. Leadership ownership and execution boundaries are not yet fully protected**

Execution still risks routing upward instead of surfacing where it belongs. When problems escalate to ownership by default, the operating system is not doing its job.

**2. Recruiting is understood but not yet fully predictable**

The business needs boring, repeatable recruiting rather than bursts of effort. The pace must be visible weekly and provable, not anecdotal.

**3. Marketing leverage exists but does not always compound**

The issue is not activity. The issue is whether output becomes reusable leverage that reduces individual effort over time.

**4. Operations can absorb upstream failures instead of exposing them**

When recruiting is slow or marketing doesn't deliver, ops absorbs the pressure instead of the failure surfacing where it occurred. That creates fake health and delays structural correction.

**5. Retention leverage must be made more explicit**

The system should clarify what specific retention activities actually move the business week to week, not just track attrition after the fact.

**6. Leadership rescue is still a default failure mode**

If the company depends on escalation to ownership to keep moving, the operating system is not doing its job. Problems should surface and resolve at the department level.
