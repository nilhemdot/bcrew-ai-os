# ATTRACT Avatar Profiles: Agent Recruiting
## Benson Crew Real Estate Team | Real Brokerage, Ontario, Canada

Research compiled: 2026-04-02
Purpose: Deep avatar profiles for ATTRACT (recruiting) content marketing

## AVATAR 1: THE STUCK PRODUCER

**Tagline:** "I'm doing everything alone and plateauing."

### 1. Demographics & Psychographics

- Age: 28-42
- Experience: 3-8 years licensed
- Annual production: 3-15 deals/year
- Estimated GCI: $30K-$90K (Ontario median is ~$45K)
- Personality: Hardworking, independent, stubborn about asking for help. Prides themselves on doing it "their way" but privately knows it's not working well enough.
- Values: Autonomy, fair compensation, self-reliance. Wants to be seen as successful.
- Career stage: Past survival mode but not thriving. Has enough business to stay in the game but not enough to build real wealth.
- Identity: "I'm a real estate agent" (not yet "I run a real estate business").

### 2. Pain Points & Triggers

**What keeps them up at night:**
- Inconsistent income. Good months followed by dry spells.
- Watching newer agents on social media who seem to be crushing it.
- Feeling like they should be further along at this point in their career.
- No systems. Every deal feels like reinventing the wheel.
- The math: paying $500-$2,000/month in desk fees, MLS fees, marketing costs, and association dues while netting $30-50K.

**Trigger events that push them to consider switching:**
- A particularly bad month (zero closings) after years of grinding.
- Seeing a peer who switched brokerages and visibly leveled up.
- Attending a conference or webinar where someone describes systems they don't have.
- Getting recruited by another brokerage and realizing other options exist.
- Losing a listing to an agent on a team with better marketing materials.
- Tax season: seeing how much they actually made vs. what they thought.

### 3. Actual Search Queries

What they type into Google:
- "how to grow real estate business"
- "real estate agent stuck not growing"
- "best brokerage for mid-level agents"
- "how to get more real estate leads without cold calling"
- "real estate coaching programs worth it"
- "should I join a real estate team or stay solo"
- "how to build systems as a solo real estate agent"
- "why am I not closing more deals real estate"
- "real estate agent income plateau"
- "commission split vs desk fees which is better"

What they search on Reddit/forums:
- "Anyone else feel stuck doing 5-6 deals a year?"
- "Is it worth switching brokerages after 5 years?"
- "How do you break through the plateau?"
- "Solo agent vs joining a team pros cons"

### 4. Language They Use (Real Agent Voices)

**How they describe frustration:**
- "I feel like I'm spinning my wheels."
- "I'm doing everything I can, but I can't take on any more clients without burning out."
- "I know I should be doing more but I don't know what to prioritize."
- "My brokerage doesn't really offer anything beyond a desk and a logo."
- "I'm paying all these fees but what am I actually getting?"
- "I see other agents posting about their systems and CRMs and I'm still using spreadsheets."
- "I don't have a lead gen system. I'm basically waiting for referrals and hoping."
- "The training they promised when I joined was one orientation session and then crickets."

**How they talk about what they want:**
- "I just want someone to show me what's working."
- "I need a proven system, not another motivational seminar."
- "I want to work smarter, not harder. I'm already working hard enough."
- "I need accountability. Nobody at my brokerage cares if I close or not."

### 5. Content That Moves Them

**Formats that work:**
- Before/after case studies: "Sarah was doing 6 deals/year. 18 months later she's at 22." (with real numbers)
- "Day in the life" content showing the systems and support in action
- Comparison content: "What 5 deals/year looks like solo vs. on our team"
- Income breakdowns that show net (after fees) not gross
- Short-form video: agent testimonials talking about what changed when they joined

**Proof they need:**
- Real production numbers (not vague "top team" claims)
- Agent testimonials from people who were in their exact situation
- Specific systems walkthrough (CRM, lead routing, transaction coordination)
- Net income comparison: their current split with fees vs. team split with support
- Evidence that joining doesn't mean losing autonomy

**What does NOT work:**
- "Unlimited leads!" (they've heard this lie before)
- Vague promises about culture
- Recruiting presentations heavy on company history and light on agent results
- Generic motivational content

### 6. Platform Behavior

- **Facebook:** Member of 2-5 agent groups. Lurks more than posts. Reads complaint threads. May be in local board groups.
- **Instagram:** Follows real estate coaches (Tom Ferry, etc.) and successful agents. Compares their content to theirs and feels behind.
- **YouTube:** Watches "how to grow real estate business" content. Consumes training videos at 2AM when they can't sleep.
- **LinkedIn:** Has a profile but doesn't post much. Occasionally engages with industry content.
- **Reddit:** Reads r/realtors and r/RealEstate. May post anonymously asking for advice.

**Engagement patterns:** Most active in evenings and weekends. Consumes content during slow periods (January, summer). Engages more with peer-level content than guru content.

### 7. Objections

1. **"I'll lose my clients if I switch."** (92% of clients follow the agent, not the brokerage brand)
2. **"Rebranding is expensive. New signs, new cards, new everything."**
3. **"Better the devil you know."** Fear of the unknown. What if the new place is worse?
4. **"I don't want to give up a chunk of my commission to a team."** (Doesn't realize they'd net more on fewer deals with support)
5. **"I've been here X years, I have loyalty."** (Loyalty to a brokerage that isn't loyal back)
6. **"What if I'm just not good enough?"** (The real objection they won't say out loud: fear that the problem is them, not the brokerage)

### 8. Buying Signals

- Starts attending events outside their brokerage
- Updates LinkedIn profile or headshot
- Asks peers about their brokerage experience
- Googles other brokerages or teams in their area
- Engages with recruiting content on social media (likes, saves, doesn't comment publicly)
- Takes a coffee meeting with a team leader
- Asks specific questions about commission structure, systems, or support
- Stops showing up to brokerage meetings or events
- Starts complaining more openly about their current situation
- Reaches out to a coach or training program independently

## AVATAR 2: THE NEW AGENT

**Tagline:** "Nobody taught me how to actually do this."

### 1. Demographics & Psychographics

- Age: 24-38 (often career changers in their late 20s-30s)
- Experience: 0-2 years licensed
- Annual production: 0-3 deals/year
- Estimated GCI: $0-$25K (many are net negative after expenses)
- Personality: Optimistic at first, increasingly anxious. Embarrassed to admit they're struggling. Compares themselves to every success story they see online.
- Values: Financial independence, flexibility, being their own boss. Many entered real estate because they hated corporate jobs.
- Career stage: Survival mode. Considering quitting. May have a side job or savings runway that's running out.
- Identity: Still figuring out if they're "really" a real estate agent or if they made a mistake.

### 2. Pain Points & Triggers

**What keeps them up at night:**
- Zero pipeline. No leads. No deals in progress. Phone isn't ringing.
- Money burning: OREA licensing fees, board dues, MLS fees, desk fees, insurance, marketing spend. All outflow, zero inflow.
- Imposter syndrome: "Everyone else seems to know what they're doing."
- The gap between what licensing courses taught and what the job actually requires.
- Embarrassment: Friends and family ask "how's real estate going?" and they don't know what to say.
- Realizing their brokerage "training program" was a binder and a handshake.

**Trigger events:**
- 3+ months with no closings
- Running out of savings or needing to get a "real job" to cover expenses
- Seeing classmates from OREA who are already quitting
- A bad experience with a deal (almost closing, falling through) with no mentor to call
- Scrolling Instagram and seeing agents with teams, systems, and support while they're alone
- The realization that 50-80% of new agents quit within 2 years and they might become a statistic

### 3. Actual Search Queries

What they type into Google:
- "how to get clients as a new real estate agent"
- "best brokerage for new agents Ontario"
- "how to get leads in real estate with no money"
- "new real estate agent first year tips"
- "should I quit real estate"
- "how long does it take to make money in real estate"
- "real estate agent mentor near me"
- "OREA exam passed now what"
- "real estate team vs solo new agent"
- "how to survive first year in real estate"
- "do real estate agents get leads from their brokerage"
- "best CRM for new real estate agents free"

What they search on Reddit/forums:
- "New agent, 3 months in, no sales. Should I quit?"
- "What do you wish you knew before becoming a realtor?"
- "Is it normal to have no leads your first year?"
- "How do I get my first client?"

### 4. Language They Use (Real Agent Voices)

**How they describe their struggle:**
- "I've been licensed for 2 years now and I have not sold anything. I'm throwing more money away on fees, MLS, etc., than I am getting anything."
- "Nobody taught me how to actually prospect. The licensing course was all theory."
- "My broker said they'd train me but it was one orientation and then I was on my own."
- "I feel like I'm guessing at everything. Contracts, negotiations, prospecting, time management."
- "The promises don't match the reality. Brokerages love to recruit, but when it comes to training and mentorship... crickets."
- "I'm in constant 'yes' mode and it's not hustle anymore, it's just burning out."
- "You should expect it to take about three years before you actually have a pipeline big enough to support yourself. Nobody told me that."
- "I want to quit my real estate career but I feel ashamed because 'quitters' are seen as failures."
- "Money stress is the quickest way to send new agents running, and that's exactly where I am."
- "Most agents don't quit because they're lazy or untalented. They quit because the industry isn't set up to support them."

**How they talk about what they want:**
- "Just tell me exactly what to do every day."
- "I need a mentor who will actually pick up the phone when I have a question about a contract."
- "I want training that's practical, not theoretical."
- "Give me a proven playbook, not 'go knock on doors.'"
- "I want to shadow someone on actual deals."

### 5. Content That Moves Them

**Formats that work:**
- "New agent to X deals in 12 months" stories (with the specific steps, not just the result)
- Training content previews: "Here's a sample of what our weekly coaching looks like"
- Day-in-the-life videos of a new agent on the team (showing the support system in action)
- "What your first 90 days look like when you join our team" breakdowns
- Comparison: "Your first year solo vs. your first year with us" side by side
- FAQ-style content addressing their exact fears

**Proof they need:**
- New agents who joined the team and closed deals within 90 days
- Specific training schedule (not "we have training" but "Tuesday 10AM: prospecting workshop, Thursday 2PM: contract review")
- A named mentor or coach they'd be paired with
- Real numbers: "Our average first-year agent closes X deals. Industry average is Y."
- That they won't be abandoned after onboarding

**What does NOT work:**
- Flashy income claims ("Make $100K your first year!")
- Content aimed at experienced agents (feels exclusionary)
- "Entrepreneurial spirit" messaging (they need structure, not more freedom)
- Guru content that makes them feel worse about where they are

### 6. Platform Behavior

- **Facebook:** Active in new agent groups ("New Real Estate Agent Support Group" type pages). Posts questions, reads answers, takes comfort in knowing others are struggling too.
- **Instagram:** Follows real estate coaches, motivational content, "how to" accounts. Saves a lot of posts. Watches Reels about scripts, lead gen, and mindset.
- **YouTube:** Heavy consumer. Searches for tutorials, day-in-the-life content, and "how I got my first client" videos. Watches at night.
- **TikTok:** May consume real estate content here. Short-form "real talk about being a new agent" resonates.
- **Reddit:** Lurks r/realtors, occasionally posts asking for advice. Reads success stories for hope.

**Engagement patterns:** Most active in the morning (before the guilt of "I should be prospecting" kicks in) and late at night. Consumes massive amounts of content but struggles to implement.

### 7. Objections

1. **"I can't afford to lose any more commission to a team split."** (Doesn't realize 50% of something is better than 100% of nothing)
2. **"I should probably give my current brokerage more time."** (The sunk cost fallacy)
3. **"I'm not experienced enough for a real team."** (Self-selection out based on imposter syndrome)
4. **"What if I join a team and still fail? Then I'll really know it's me."** (Fear of proving they're not cut out for it)
5. **"Teams take your leads and give you the scraps."** (Bad experiences they've heard about)
6. **"I just got my license with this brokerage. Won't it look bad to leave so soon?"**

### 8. Buying Signals

- Searches for "should I quit real estate" (they're at the decision point)
- Joins multiple agent groups on Facebook seeking community they don't have
- Asks other agents "what brokerage are you with?" at open houses or events
- DMs agents on Instagram who seem happy at their brokerage/team
- Watches YouTube videos about specific brokerages or teams
- Starts comparing team models and commission structures
- Mentions to friends/family that they're "thinking about their options"
- Attends a team's open house event or webinar
- Responds to a recruiting ad (they usually ignore these unless desperate)

## AVATAR 3: THE EXPERIENCED AGENT (CEILING HITTER)

**Tagline:** "I need a team behind me so I can scale."

### 1. Demographics & Psychographics

- Age: 35-55
- Experience: 8-20+ years licensed
- Annual production: 15-40+ deals/year (50+ lifetime)
- Estimated GCI: $150K-$400K+
- Personality: Driven, competent, proud. Has built everything themselves. Control freak tendencies because they've been burned by delegating before. Privately exhausted.
- Values: Excellence, client experience, reputation, income. Starting to value time and freedom more as they age.
- Career stage: Peak earning but unsustainable pace. Working 60-80 hour weeks. Missing family time. Body is showing signs of stress.
- Identity: "I AM my business." This is the core problem. They can't separate themselves from every transaction.

### 2. Pain Points & Triggers

**What keeps them up at night:**
- Working 70+ hours/week with no end in sight. Vacation means lost income.
- Being the bottleneck for every single thing in their business.
- Knowing they can't grow without help but not knowing how to hire or delegate.
- Fear that nobody will care about their clients the way they do.
- The "hit by a bus" scenario: if they stop, everything stops. No business equity.
- Watching team leaders in their market who do fewer deals personally but make more money.

**Trigger events:**
- A health scare or family ultimatum ("You're never home.")
- Missing a child's event because of a showing or closing
- Having multiple deals fall through simultaneously and handling all the fallout alone
- Seeing a peer build a team and start working fewer hours while earning more
- Burnout episode: the phone rings and they feel dread instead of excitement
- Attempting to take a vacation and spending it on their phone managing deals

### 3. Actual Search Queries

What they type into Google:
- "how to scale real estate business without burnout"
- "hiring a transaction coordinator real estate"
- "solo agent to team leader transition"
- "best real estate teams to join as experienced agent"
- "how to delegate in real estate"
- "real estate virtual assistant worth it"
- "real estate business systems and leverage"
- "should top producer join a team"
- "how to build a real estate team from scratch"
- "real estate leverage Gary Keller model"

What they search on Reddit/forums:
- "Top producers: how do you take vacations?"
- "Thinking about joining a team as a senior agent. Worth it?"
- "How did you go from solo to team?"
- "Anyone else working 80 hours and still feeling behind?"

### 4. Language They Use (Real Agent Voices)

**How they describe their situation:**
- "I'm doing everything I can, but I can't take on any more clients without burning out."
- "I'm essentially one person doing the work of 3 or 4 people."
- "Growth quickly turns into burnout when you have no leverage."
- "I know I need to delegate but every time I've tried, the quality drops and clients complain."
- "My family is tired of coming second to my business."
- "I can't take a week off without everything falling apart."
- "I don't have a business. I have a job that owns me."
- "I'm a $300K earner trapped in a $300K prison."
- "The scariest part isn't the work. It's knowing that if I stop, it all stops."

**How they talk about what they want:**
- "I need someone to handle the stuff that doesn't require my face."
- "I want to go from doing 30 deals myself to doing 15 and having my team do 50."
- "I want to build something with actual equity, not just a commission stream."
- "I need to work ON my business, not IN it."
- "I want the back office handled. Admin, marketing, transaction coordination. I'll do the client-facing work."

### 5. Content That Moves Them

**Formats that work:**
- Case studies of experienced agents who joined and immediately got leverage (admin, TC, marketing)
- "How our top producer went from 35 solo deals to 60 team deals while working fewer hours"
- Detailed breakdown of the support infrastructure: who does what, how it works
- Income comparison that accounts for time: "$300K in 80 hrs/week vs. $350K in 50 hrs/week"
- Long-form content: podcasts, YouTube interviews with other experienced agents on the team
- One-on-one conversations (this avatar doesn't respond to mass marketing)

**Proof they need:**
- Specific support staff they'd have access to (named people, not "support available")
- A clear value proposition for what they give up (split) vs. what they get (leverage)
- Team production numbers showing the multiplier effect
- Testimonials from other experienced agents who made the same leap
- Evidence of quality control (they won't risk their reputation)
- Culture proof: "We don't micromanage. You run your business, we handle the rest."

**What does NOT work:**
- Entry-level recruiting messaging
- "Join our family!" culture-heavy messaging without substance
- Revenue share pitches (they care about direct income, not MLM-adjacent models)
- Anything that feels like a demotion from their current status

### 6. Platform Behavior

- **LinkedIn:** Most active here. Posts about market insights, client wins. Connected to other top producers. This is where they network professionally.
- **Facebook:** May be in private mastermind or industry groups. Less active in general agent groups.
- **Instagram:** Uses it for business (property marketing) but doesn't engage much with recruiting content.
- **YouTube:** Watches business growth content (not just real estate). Gary Vee, business podcasts, scaling strategies.
- **Events/Conferences:** More likely to be at industry events (TRREB, OREA conferences) than online. Personal connections matter most.

**Engagement patterns:** Limited social media time. If they engage with your content, it's a strong signal. Most decisions happen through personal referrals and one-on-one conversations, not content consumption.

### 7. Objections

1. **"I've built my brand as a solo agent. Joining a team is a step backward."** (Ego/identity objection. Most powerful one.)
2. **"I don't want to give up 30-50% of my commission."** (Doesn't see the leverage math yet)
3. **"My clients are loyal to ME. A team dilutes that."** (Partially true; needs to see how team enhances, not replaces)
4. **"I've seen teams where the agents do all the work and the leader takes the cut."** (Bad team experiences in the market)
5. **"I tried hiring before and it was a disaster."** (Previous failed attempt at building their own team)
6. **"What about my personal brand? Would I have to rebrand?"**

### 8. Buying Signals

- Starts asking other top producers about their team structure
- Attends an event or webinar they wouldn't normally attend
- Brings up "leverage" or "scaling" in conversations
- Mentions burnout, exhaustion, or work-life balance concerns to peers
- Starts researching transaction coordinator services independently
- Asks specific operational questions: "How does your team handle [X]?"
- Takes a one-on-one meeting with a team leader (this is a MAJOR signal for this avatar)
- Starts talking about building equity or "what happens when I retire"
- Explores team models at their current brokerage before looking externally

## AVATAR 4: THE TEAM LEADER

**Tagline:** "I can't build the systems my team needs."

### 1. Demographics & Psychographics

- Age: 32-50
- Experience: 7-15+ years licensed, 1-5 years running a team
- Team size: 2-8 agents under them
- Annual team production: 20-80+ deals
- Estimated personal GCI: $100K-$300K (but expenses eat into it heavily)
- Personality: Ambitious, entrepreneurial, builder mentality. Wants to lead. May be better at selling than managing. Often overwhelmed by the operational side of running a team.
- Values: Growth, leadership, building something bigger than themselves. Legacy.
- Career stage: Has proven they can sell. Now learning (the hard way) that leading is a completely different skill set.
- Identity: "Team leader" but often feels like "the agent who does everything plus manages other agents."

### 2. Pain Points & Triggers

**What keeps them up at night:**
- Their agents aren't producing. Recruited them, can't train them, can't retain them.
- No real systems: no CRM that works for the team, no standardized training, no onboarding process.
- They're still doing most of the deals themselves plus managing the team.
- Agent turnover: losing agents they trained to other teams or brokerages.
- The math: team overhead (splits to agents, tools, marketing, admin) makes their take-home less than when they were solo.
- Competing with other teams that have better infrastructure, tech, and brand presence.

**Trigger events:**
- Losing a top agent to a better-resourced team
- Realizing their "team" is really just them with helpers, not a real organization
- Seeing a team at a conference with proper systems and feeling embarrassed at their own setup
- A failed hire or agent who underperforms because they couldn't train them properly
- Getting overwhelmed managing multiple agents' deal problems simultaneously
- Calculating that their per-hour rate is worse than when they were solo

### 3. Actual Search Queries

What they type into Google:
- "how to build a real estate team structure"
- "best CRM for real estate teams"
- "real estate team training program template"
- "how to retain agents on my team"
- "real estate team leader coaching"
- "real estate team operating systems"
- "team commission split models real estate"
- "how to create SOPs for real estate team"
- "real estate team cap model vs split model"
- "joining a mega team vs running my own team"
- "Real Brokerage team structure revenue share"

What they search on Reddit/forums:
- "Team leaders: what CRM do you use?"
- "How do you train new agents on your team?"
- "Is it worth running a team or just producing yourself?"
- "How do you structure commission splits for team agents?"

### 4. Language They Use (Real Agent Voices)

**How they describe their frustration:**
- "I spent more time managing problems than selling this month."
- "I'm paying for a CRM nobody on my team actually uses."
- "Every time I train someone, they leave. I'm basically running a free coaching program for my competitors."
- "Selling homes and leading a team require two different skill sets. I'm good at one and learning the other the hard way."
- "I can't compete with teams that have admin support, marketing departments, and training programs. I'm one person trying to do all of that."
- "My team needs systems but I don't even know where to start."
- "Without a CRM, we rely on memory, inboxes, and spreadsheets. It leads to mistakes and missed opportunities."
- "I recruited 5 agents last year and 3 of them did nothing."

**How they talk about what they want:**
- "I need an operating system for my team, not just another tool."
- "I want to plug into infrastructure that already works."
- "I want to focus on coaching and selling, not building tech stacks from scratch."
- "I need a training program I can hand to new agents on day one."
- "Revenue share would let me retain agents who outgrow my team structure."

### 5. Content That Moves Them

**Formats that work:**
- Deep-dive content on team structure and economics within Benson Crew / Real Brokerage
- "How our team within Real Brokerage works" breakdown (revenue share, cap, support)
- Case studies of other team leaders who brought their team over and scaled
- Operational content: "Here's the tech stack, the training calendar, the onboarding checklist"
- Peer-to-peer conversations with other team leaders in the organization
- Podcast or long-form interview: "Why I moved my team to Benson Crew"

**Proof they need:**
- Specific infrastructure they'd get access to (CRM, training program, admin support, marketing)
- Revenue share math: "Bring your 5 agents, here's what that looks like financially"
- Agent retention data: "Our team retention rate is X% vs. industry average of Y%"
- The model: exactly how their team would operate within the larger team structure
- That they maintain leadership over their agents (not getting absorbed and losing their identity)
- Evidence that the team leader role is respected and supported, not just a recruiter title

**What does NOT work:**
- Individual agent recruiting messaging (they need team-level conversations)
- "Join us as an agent" (they're thinking as a team, not an individual)
- Overly simplistic revenue share pitches without the operational substance
- Anything that suggests they'd lose control of their team

### 6. Platform Behavior

- **LinkedIn:** Active. Posts about team building, leadership. Follows other team leaders and real estate business content.
- **Facebook:** Active in team leader specific groups. May run their own team's Facebook group.
- **YouTube:** Watches team-building content, leadership development, business systems videos.
- **Instagram:** Uses it for team branding and agent recruiting themselves. Studies what other successful teams post.
- **Events:** Attends conferences, masterminds, coaching events (Tom Ferry, Brian Buffini, etc.). In-person relationships drive decisions.

**Engagement patterns:** Evaluates content through the lens of "can I use this for my team?" Higher bar for engagement but deeper when they do engage. Prefers private conversations over public interaction.

### 7. Objections

1. **"I've built my team. I don't want to give up control."** (The #1 objection. Must be addressed head-on.)
2. **"What happens to my agents if I join? Do they answer to me or to Benson Crew?"**
3. **"I don't want to be absorbed into someone else's brand."**
4. **"The economics need to make sense for my whole team, not just me."**
5. **"I've been burned by brokerage promises before. Why would this be different?"**
6. **"What if my agents want to leave my sub-team? Do they stay at Real or leave entirely?"**
7. **"I don't want to pay cap on top of running team expenses."**

### 8. Buying Signals

- Starts asking detailed questions about team-within-team structures
- Researches Real Brokerage revenue share model specifically
- Attends a Benson Crew event or Real Brokerage team event
- Asks about specific tools and infrastructure (CRM, training, admin)
- Calculates the revenue share math for their specific team size
- Reaches out to other team leaders who've made a similar move
- Mentions frustration with building infrastructure in conversations
- Starts comparing their current brokerage's team support vs. what they've heard about
- Asks "What would it look like if I brought my team over?"

## AVATAR 5: THE BROKERAGE REFUGEE

**Tagline:** "I know what I DON'T want. Show me different."

### 1. Demographics & Psychographics

- Age: 28-50 (widest range; this happens at every career stage)
- Experience: 2-15+ years licensed
- Annual production: Varies widely (5-30+ deals)
- Estimated GCI: $40K-$250K+
- Personality: Skeptical. Burned. Defensive. Has a long list of red flags they watch for now. More analytical than emotional in their next decision because emotions got them into the last bad situation.
- Values: Transparency, honesty, proof over promises. Values actions over words.
- Career stage: In transition. Recently left or actively planning exit from current brokerage.
- Identity: "Survivor." They know what they're worth now because they've seen what happens when a brokerage doesn't value them.

### 2. Pain Points & Triggers

**What keeps them up at night:**
- Feeling betrayed by promises that never materialized.
- Wasted time: months or years at a brokerage that didn't deliver.
- Money lost to hidden fees, desk fees, franchise fees, technology fees, "marketing contributions."
- Worry about making the same mistake again with a new brokerage.
- The vulnerability of being "in between" - no brokerage support, no brand, uncertain.

**What they experienced at their previous brokerage (common complaints by brand):**

**RE/MAX complaints:**
- Desk fees ranging from several hundred to $2,000+/month
- "Paying outrageous desk fees because the broker wasn't even providing a desk"
- High franchise fees on top of commission splits
- 95/5 plan with desk fees or RAPP plan with $23,000 cap
- Agents feel like they're subsidizing the franchise brand

**Keller Williams complaints:**
- "$28,000 a year for being an agent, $500/month for an office. $34,000 annually."
- Multiple layers of management all taking cuts
- Profit Share vs Revenue Share: "KW only shared a couple hundred dollars total vs $51K from eXp Revenue Share"
- Promised exclusivity and support, didn't deliver
- Culture of recruiting over production
- "Training" is often "teach the MREA model" on repeat

**Royal LePage complaints:**
- High franchise fees
- Brand perception issues (seen as "old school" by younger agents)
- Limited technology innovation
- Agent churn from leadership decisions made without agent consultation
- 1,200 agents moved out of Royal LePage in 2025 when ownership switched brands without asking agents

**eXp complaints:**
- Over-focus on recruitment/revenue share over actual selling
